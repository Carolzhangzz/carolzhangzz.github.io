Incoming CS Ph.D. @ FSU

Hi, I’m Carol Zhang. I’m an incoming CS Ph.D. student at Florida State University, where I will be advised by Prof. Yifang Wang. I received my M.S. in Computer Science from the University of California, San Diego (UCSD), and my B.S. from China Jiliang University (CJLU) & Auckland University of Technology (AUT).

My main research interests focus on AI for Social Good and AI Agents. I have designed and developed conversational agents leveraging NLP techniques for behavior analysis and intervention.

Previously, I have worked with Prof. Steven Dow at UCSD Design Lab, and with Dr. Ray LC at HK CityU, resulting in a co-first authored paper published at CHI’24 and a first-authored paper at CHI’25.

I am happy to collaborate or just chat about research, my email is qiz065@ucsd.edu.

Serious games offer a promising solution by creating artificial environments where players can immediately experience the outcomes of their actions. To explore this potential, we developed EcoEcho, a GenAI-powered game leveraging multimodal agents to raise sustainability awareness. These agents engage players in natural conversations, prompting them to take in-game actions that lead to visible environmental impacts. We evaluated EcoEcho using a mixed-methods approach with 23 participants. Results show a significant increase in intended sustainable behaviors post-game, although attitudes towards sustainability had only marginal effects, suggesting that in-game actions likely can motivate intended real world behaviors despite similar opinions on sustainability.
